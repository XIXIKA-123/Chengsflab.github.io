Research

The intrinsic and extrinsic effects of TET proteins during gastrulation

This work demonstrates an unbiased approach for defining intrinsic and extrinsic embryonic gene function based on temporal differentiation atlases and disentangles the intracellular effects of the demethylation machinery from its broader tissue-level ramifications.

Cell. 185, 3169-3185.

Positions

Post-doctoral position

Job Responsibilities :

  • 1. Undertake or participate in research projects and apply for postdoctoral research funding;
  • 2. Develop research plans and promote project progress, analyze experimental data, and write reports and papers;
  • 3. Develop new experimental techniques related to the research group's focus areas;
  • 4. Participate in the scientific training of group members.

Job Requirements :

  • After complete doctoral research training, possessing or soon to obtain a PhD in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, Developmental Biology, Stem Cell Biology, Bioinformatics, or related fields;
  • Having a background in epigenetics research, with experience in mammalian stem cell culture, flow cytometry sorting, and high-throughput sequencing;
  • Strong self-learning ability and problem-solving skills, unafraid of challenges, and willing to experiment;
  • Experience in embryo dissection, gene editing and screening, adenovirus or lentivirus packaging, or a background in computational biology will be given preference.
